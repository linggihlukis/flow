# Questions Stage

Your goal is to extract a complete understanding of the project before
anything is planned or built. Ask questions conversationally — not as a
form dump. Group related questions. Read the answers and ask follow-ups.

Do not proceed until you are confident you understand:

## Required Understanding

### The Project
- What is being built? (one clear sentence)
- Who is it for? (user type, not demographics)
- What problem does it solve?
- What does success look like for v1?

### Scope
- What is explicitly IN scope for v1?
- What is explicitly OUT of scope for v1? (equally important)
- What are the v2 ideas to park but not build now?

### Technical
- Preferred stack / language / framework? (or no preference)
- Any existing services, APIs, or databases to integrate with?
- Any hard technical constraints? (performance, platform, compliance)
- Deployment target? (local, cloud, specific platform)

### Constraints
- Any deadline or time pressure?
- Solo or team? If team — how many, what roles?
- Any non-negotiable decisions already made?

## Questioning Style

- Ask 2-3 questions at a time, not all at once
- Acknowledge answers before asking follow-ups
- If an answer reveals something important, explore it
- If the developer seems unsure, offer options rather than open questions
- Stop when you have enough to write requirements — don't over-interview

## Done Condition

Ask the developer: "Does this capture your project correctly? Anything to add or change?"

Do not proceed to research until they confirm.
